## 1. Executive overview

Our managed AI voice and automation service provides a professional digital front desk that can communicate with customers, answer business questions, handle supported transactions, and connect customer conversations to your operational systems.

The service can be configured for restaurants, hospitality businesses, clinics, appointment-based companies, service providers, retail businesses, and multi-location organizations. The exact conversation flows, integrations, languages, calling requirements, and reporting are configured around each client’s business.

This is a managed service rather than a simple chatbot subscription. It includes implementation, deployment, system integrations, monitoring, quality testing, continuing optimization, and technical support.

## 2. What the AI agent can do

Depending on the agreed scope, the agent can:

- Answer frequently asked questions using approved business information.
- Explain products, services, menus, prices, policies, branches, and operating hours.
- Take orders, bookings, reservations, or appointment requests.
- Modify or cancel supported orders and reservations.
- Collect the information required to complete a customer request.
- Confirm transaction details before performing an action.
- Send confirmations through supported channels.
- Update a database, CRM, reservation system, calendar, or operational workflow.
- Transfer or escalate a conversation when human assistance is required.
- Communicate in approved languages and mixed-language conversation styles.
- Provide conversation summaries, outcomes, and management reporting.

The final capability list is confirmed during discovery. An action is considered supported only when it is documented, integrated, tested, and included in the signed scope.

## 3. How the commercial model works

The commercial structure has four components:

1. **One-time setup and implementation fee** — pays for discovery, design, configuration, integrations, testing, training, and production launch.
2. **Monthly managed-service fee** — pays for the live AI service, infrastructure, hosting, automation, monitoring, maintenance, optimization, reporting, and support.
3. **Usage allowance and overage** — each plan contains a defined number of AI conversation minutes. Additional usage is billed according to the agreement.
4. **Optional and variable services** — international telephony, messaging, special integrations, custom development, and third-party subscriptions are quoted separately where applicable.

This structure keeps routine monthly costs predictable while allowing the service to scale with the client’s actual business volume.

## 4. What is an AI conversation minute?

An AI minute is a minute during which a customer is connected to the AI agent. It generally includes customer speech, the agent’s speech, normal conversational pauses, and time required to complete supported actions during the connected session.

The number of customers supported depends on average conversation length:

| Monthly AI minutes | Two-minute conversations | Four-minute conversations | Six-minute conversations |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 300 | 150 | 75 | 50 |
| 1,000 | 500 | 250 | 166 |
| 2,500 | 1,250 | 625 | 416 |
| 6,000 | 3,000 | 1,500 | 1,000 |
| 15,000 | 7,500 | 3,750 | 2,500 |

Actual usage depends on customer behavior, complexity, languages, order size, integrations, waiting time, and whether conversations are completed, transferred, or abandoned.

## 8. Telephone service

Standard global packages can include one United States local telephone number and US inbound calling up to the package’s AI-minute allowance.

Telephone pricing can change significantly according to:

- Country of the telephone number.
- Country and network being called.
- Inbound versus outbound calling.
- Local, mobile, toll-free, or international destination.
- Call recording or transcription requirements.
- Call transfer duration.
- Regulatory and number-registration requirements.

International calling and high-cost destinations are normally billed separately at the applicable carrier rate plus the administration percentage stated in the agreement. For some countries, a local carrier or client-owned SIP provider may be more cost-effective than an international cloud carrier.

## 9. Usage, overage, and upgrades

Usage is monitored throughout the billing period. Clients can receive notifications when usage reaches agreed thresholds, normally 70%, 85%, and 100%.

The service should not unexpectedly stop during important customer calls. Unless the contract specifies a hard cap, additional usage continues and is charged as overage.

Standard AI-minute overage guidance:

| Package | Additional AI minute |
|---|---:|
| Pilot | $0.25 |
| Launch | $0.22 |
| Growth | $0.20 |
| Scale | $0.18 |
| Enterprise | Contracted volume rate |

If usage remains above 120% of the package allowance for two consecutive months, moving to the next package is normally more economical.

Telephone carrier usage and AI-service overage are different charges. The proposal will state whether carrier minutes are included, passed through, or supplied through a client-owned account.

The final setup or monthly price may change because of:

- Peak simultaneous call requirements.
- Average conversation length.
- Number of branches, locations, brands, or departments.
- Number of languages and voice profiles.
- Complexity of orders, reservations, appointments, or support cases.
- Number and quality of external integrations.
- Condition and structure of existing business data.

- Outbound calling, SMS, WhatsApp, or marketing workflows.
- Call recording, transcription, retention, or analytics requirements.
- Custom dashboards and management reports.
- Data isolation, security, audit, SSO, or compliance requirements.
- Support hours, response targets, and service-level agreement.
- On-site training, travel, or dedicated staff.

Price changes are discussed and approved before work outside the agreed scope begins.

## 13. Implementation process

### Phase 1 — Discovery

We document customer intents, business rules, supported actions, data, integrations, languages, escalation paths, and success measures.

### Phase 2 — Design and configuration

We configure the agent, knowledge, workflows, systems, security, dashboards, and telephone routing included in the scope.

### Phase 3 — Testing

We test normal conversations, ambiguous questions, interruptions, corrections, mixed-language speech, tool failures, duplicate-action protection, and escalation behavior.

### Phase 4 — Client acceptance

The client reviews agreed scenarios and approves the service for production.

### Phase 5 — Production launch

The service is deployed, monitored, and initially tuned using real customer behavior.

### Phase 6 — Continuing improvement

Usage, quality, failures, and business outcomes are reviewed. Approved improvements are released according to the service plan.

Standard implementations may take two to six weeks. Enterprise, multi-location, or legacy-system projects can require a longer implementation plan.
