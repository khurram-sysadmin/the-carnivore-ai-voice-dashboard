# AI Voice Agent Agency Business Model

Research and planning date: 13 July 2026  
Product reference: The Carnivore AI Voice Dashboard  
Planning exchange rate: USD 1 = PKR 280 (editable; refresh before issuing quotations)

## Executive decision

Build this as a **vertical managed AI operations company**, beginning with restaurants and hospitality. Do not sell “an ElevenLabs agent” or cheap minutes. Sell a measurable operating result: every call or web conversation answered, orders and reservations completed, customer details captured, confirmations sent, and failures monitored.

The commercial model should have four parts:

1. Paid discovery and implementation.
2. A monthly managed-service subscription tied to business volume.
3. Transparent usage overages and country-specific telephony pass-through.
4. Paid expansion modules for additional locations, channels, integrations, and outbound campaigns.

The recommended global tiers are $497, $997, $1,997, and $4,997 per month, with setup fees from $1,500 to $12,000. Suggested Pakistan launch prices are PKR 99,000, 225,000, 450,000, and 1,100,000 per month, with lower local delivery labor but the same dollar-denominated platform costs.

At these prices, estimated direct gross margins are approximately 58%–72% on the conservative standalone cost model. A well-run multi-client portfolio should improve because ElevenLabs, n8n, Vercel, email, monitoring, and operational labor can be used more efficiently across clients. The target should be **65%–75% direct gross margin**, never below 55% without a strategic reason.

## What the company sells

### Core promise

“We install and operate a multilingual AI front desk that handles customer conversations, completes orders and reservations, updates your systems, and improves every month.”

### Initial restaurant package

- Urdu, Roman Urdu, Hindi, and English web/phone conversations.
- Menu, price, branch, policy, and availability knowledge.
- Ordering, reservation, modification, and cancellation workflows.
- Customer identity and contact context passed securely from the signed-in product.
- Email/Slack/CRM/POS notifications.
- Manager escalation when the agent is uncertain.
- Conversation transcripts, outcome tracking, and operational dashboard.
- Monthly quality review and prompt/workflow improvement.

### Expansion modules

- Public telephone number and inbound/outbound calling.
- WhatsApp/SMS confirmations and follow-ups.
- POS, CRM, calendar, payment, delivery, or loyalty integration.
- Multiple locations, languages, brands, or departments.
- Lead qualification and abandoned-order follow-up.
- Automated review requests and win-back campaigns.
- Enterprise security, audit, retention, SSO, and custom SLA.

## Global market findings

The market separates into three groups:

|---|---:|---|---|
| Self-service AI receptionist | Goodcall: $79–$249/agent/month | Software, templates, customer-count allowances | This is the low-price software anchor, not a custom managed service. |
| Managed/hybrid receptionist | Smith.ai: $300 for 30 calls; $810 for 90; $2,100 for 300 | Accountable call handling, integrations, screening, booking | Customers pay for reliability and business outcomes, not raw compute. |
| Managed AI agency | WildRun: $497/$997/$1,997 monthly, with a published $1,500 Starter setup | Discovery, scripting, integration, launch, monitoring and reports | Closely validates the recommended global packaging. |
| Developer platform | Retell: $0.07–$0.31/min; Vapi: $0.05/min plus providers; Bland: $0.11–$0.14/min plus plan fees | Infrastructure and APIs | Raw platform cost is only one component of service COGS. |
| Enterprise platform | Synthflow: enterprise from $30,000/year | Volume, governance, concurrency, security and support | Enterprise buyers accept materially higher prices for controls and accountability. |
| Custom AI development | Clutch: common $24–$49/hour; reviewed projects often $10,000–$49,999 | Bespoke discovery and engineering | Strong setup fees are normal for real custom AI work. |

White-label providers also reveal the agency margin structure. My AI Front Desk publicly describes a $54.99 wholesale example being resold above $400. The difference is not deception: the reseller is packaging configuration, brand, integration, support, billing, and accountability. Our product is more custom and should command a higher implementation fee and retainer.

### How successful providers scale

1. **Niche first.** They sell a repeatable result to one industry rather than a generic chatbot to everyone.
2. **Productized onboarding.** Discovery forms, templates, knowledge schemas, test sets, launch gates, and runbooks reduce deployment time.
3. **Setup plus recurring revenue.** Setup pays for engineering; the subscription funds hosting, monitoring, QA, optimization, support, and continuing value.
4. **Usage boundaries.** Plans include a fair-use unit—minutes, calls, customers, or executions—with overage pricing.
5. **White-label and client access.** Dashboards, reports, and branded portals make value visible and reduce support friction.
6. **Land and expand.** Start with one location/use case, then sell more branches, channels, integrations, languages, and outbound workflows.
7. **Partner distribution.** POS vendors, CRM consultants, marketing agencies, telecom providers, and industry associations can resell or refer.
8. **Outcome reporting.** Retention grows when clients see completed orders, captured leads, saved hours, conversion, and estimated revenue—not “AI usage.”

### Contract structure

- Launch: three-month minimum.
- Growth: six-month minimum.
- Scale and Enterprise: twelve-month agreement.
- Annual prepayment: 10% recurring discount.
- Setup discount: up to 50% only for a twelve-month commitment; do not give away discovery and integration.
- Additional location: 35%–60% of the base tier depending on how much menu, logic, data, and reporting can be shared.
- Custom change work: prepaid 10-, 25-, or 50-hour packs, or a fixed-scope project.
- Carrier, SMS/WhatsApp, payment, and unusual third-party fees: pass through at cost plus 15%–20% administration, or let the client contract directly.

### Margin rules

- Quote a minimum 65% planned direct gross margin for standard deployments.
- Enterprise can begin at 55%–60% if the annual contract, expansion potential, and setup margin are strong.
- Add a 10% platform contingency and a test-call allowance to every quote.
- If a client demands unlimited use, price it from the 95th-percentile expected volume plus a 30% risk buffer and a fair-use clause.
- Reprice when actual usage is over 120% of allowance for two consecutive months.
- Keep 5%–8% of recurring revenue as a service-recovery and R&D reserve.

## Technology cost model

### ElevenLabs Agents

Current public plans are $6 for 75 minutes, $22 for 275, $99 for 1,238, $299 for 3,738, and $990 for 12,375, with additional minutes at $0.08. Concurrency rises from 6 to 40 on those tiers. LLM and telephony are separate.

Efficient use:

- Keep the system prompt focused; move large stable facts to the knowledge base.
- Use deterministic tools and workflows for transactions instead of asking the model to “reason” about database changes.
- Detect silence, voicemail, and disconnected calls quickly.
- Set usage and concurrency alerts; burst minutes cost more.
- Use simulation tests before production, then test changed paths only.
- Pool agents at the workspace level only when governance and customer contracts allow it; track cost per client internally.

### Gemini 2.5 Flash

Google lists $0.30 per million text/image/video input tokens and $2.50 per million output tokens. ElevenLabs’ agent calculator has shown a very small per-minute estimate for Gemini 2.5 Flash relative to voice hosting. The product also uses Gemini directly for app chat, so token budgets still matter.

Efficient use:

- Keep outputs concise and cap maximum output tokens.
- Send structured summaries rather than full transcript history where possible.
- Cache menu, branch, and policy context.
- Use Flash-Lite for offline tagging, classification, or summaries where quality tests pass.
- Use the strongest model only for difficult escalations, not every turn.

### Supabase

Pro starts at $25/month with the first micro compute effectively covered by included compute credit; additional projects start from $10 before other usage. It includes 100,000 MAU, 8 GB database disk, 250 GB egress, 100 GB file storage, daily backups, and seven-day logs.

Efficient use:

- Use one properly isolated production project per premium/regulated customer; do not sacrifice security to save $10–$25.
- Store structured order and conversation outcomes, not unnecessary raw audio forever.
- Define retention periods and archive old transcripts.
- Add indexes for common dashboard filters and avoid unbounded queries.
- Set row-level security and separate service credentials.

### n8n

n8n Cloud pricing is based on completed workflow executions, not individual steps. Public annual prices include €20/month for 2,500 executions and €50/month for 10,000, with unlimited workflows/users on paid plans.

Efficient use:

- Trigger once per business event; avoid frequent polling.
- Put several necessary steps inside one workflow execution.
- Use idempotency keys so retries do not duplicate orders or reservations.
- Separate client credentials and workflows logically; export/version production workflows.
- Track executions per completed order/reservation, not merely per call.
- Move to self-hosting only when execution savings exceed DevOps, security, backup, and on-call costs.

### Vercel

Pro is $20/month and includes $20 in usage credit. It is the appropriate baseline for commercial production; Hobby is not the commercial operating model.

Efficient use:

- Cache safe reads and serve static assets through the CDN.
- Put spend alerts and hard budgets in place.
- Avoid long-running synchronous work; send it to n8n/background workflows.
- Keep separate projects per client while using one agency team where contracts allow.

### Transactional email

Resend’s public pricing includes 3,000 emails/month free and $20/month for 50,000, with $0.90 per extra thousand. It is inexpensive compared with support risk.

Efficient use:

- Use one transactional provider rather than personal Gmail automation.
- Configure SPF, DKIM, DMARC, bounce suppression, and delivery webhooks.
- Send a single well-designed confirmation and only necessary status updates.

### Telephony

Twilio US local numbers are publicly priced at $1.15/month, with $0.0085/min inbound and $0.014/min outbound. Pakistan outbound is much higher: roughly $0.155/min to landlines and $0.18/min to mobile on the public page, while local-number availability and regulatory requirements vary.

Therefore:

- Never advertise one worldwide all-inclusive telephone-minute price.
- Use client-owned carrier accounts or itemized carrier pass-through.
- For Pakistan, negotiate a local SIP/BYOC provider before selling high-volume outbound service.
- Keep web voice as the lowest-cost entry package.

## Monthly support product

| Tier | QA sample | Optimization | Included changes | Critical response target | Review |
|---|---:|---|---:|---|---|
| Launch | 10 conversations | One tune/month | 1 hour | Next business day | Quarterly |
| Growth | 25 conversations | Two cycles/month | 3 hours | 8 business hours | Monthly |
| Scale | 75 + every failed action | Weekly | 6 hours | 4 hours | Monthly |
| Enterprise | Risk-based + automated evaluation | Continuous | 12 hours | 1-hour Sev-1 acknowledgement | Monthly + quarterly roadmap |

Every monthly report should show:

- Conversations handled and containment rate.
- Orders/reservations/qualified leads completed.
- Task-success and tool-success rates.
- Transfer and abandonment rates.
- Estimated revenue captured and human hours saved.
- Average latency and top customer intents.
- Top failures and fixes shipped.
- Allowance used, forecast, and recommended tier.

This reporting is the main retention mechanism: it proves that the subscription is an operating investment, not a software bill.

## Professional customer-behavior training

Do not “train” primarily by adding more prompt text. Build an evaluation and improvement system.

1. Create a taxonomy of real customer behavior: mixed language, corrections, interruptions, ambiguity, topic switches, accidental contradictions, background speech, incomplete dates, and crossed order/reservation intents.
2. Convert anonymized failures into test cases with expected intent, required fields, tool calls, and forbidden actions.
3. Maintain at least 100 golden conversations per vertical, then expand continuously.
4. Test every release against task completion, tool correctness, hallucination, privacy, latency, and customer effort.
5. Use workflow states for high-risk transactions: identify intent, collect missing fields, summarize, confirm once, execute once, and report the result.
6. Add deterministic recovery: ask one focused clarification, offer two plausible interpretations, or escalate to a human.
7. Review all failed transactions and a statistically useful sample of successful ones.
8. Track regression by language, branch, use case, noise level, and customer behavior pattern.

Quality gates before client launch:

- At least 95% correct tool selection on the golden test set.
- At least 90% end-to-end task success for supported core workflows.
- Zero duplicate order/reservation creation in retry tests.
- Zero unsupported price, menu, or policy claims in the evaluation sample.
- Clear escalation on missing data or low confidence.
- Defined latency budget and acceptable interruption behavior.

## Sales strategy

### Ideal first customer

A restaurant with one to five locations, meaningful phone/web demand, missed calls during peaks, standardized menu/processes, and an owner who can measure orders or reservations. Avoid highly bespoke businesses until the core playbook is repeatable.

### Discovery data required

- Monthly inbound calls and web conversations.
- Average call duration and peak concurrency.
- Missed/abandoned-call rate.
- Orders/reservations per month and average order value.
- Staff time and cost spent handling calls.
- Languages, locations, hours, and escalation rules.
- POS/CRM/calendar/payment systems.
- Required retention, consent, and compliance.

### ROI framing

Use the most conservative of these three values:

1. Recovered revenue from additional completed orders/reservations.
2. Saved labor hours multiplied by fully loaded staff cost.
3. Faster response and higher conversion compared with the baseline.

Aim for the monthly fee to be no more than 20%–35% of conservatively measured monthly value. For example, if the system captures $4,000 of gross profit and saves $1,000 of labor monthly, a $997 fee is easy to defend.

### Sales process

1. Ten-minute qualification call.
2. Paid discovery or structured ROI assessment.
3. Live vertical demo using the prospect’s real menu/workflow.
4. Proposal with baseline, success metrics, boundaries, and implementation plan.
5. Paid 30–45 day pilot at one location.
6. Launch review and annual expansion roadmap.

## Scale plan

### Stage 1: 0–5 clients

- Founder-led sales and delivery.
- One vertical and one core stack.
- Document every onboarding and failure.
- Obtain two quantified case studies.

### Stage 2: 6–20 clients

- Hire one implementation/QA specialist and one customer success owner.
- Standardize menus, knowledge imports, tool schemas, dashboards, and tests.
- Build centralized usage, margin, and incident monitoring.
- Establish POS/CRM/marketing-agency partnerships.

### Stage 3: 21–50 clients

- Split implementation from ongoing operations.
- Add a solution architect for complex integrations.
- Introduce partner/reseller certification and deal registration.
- Negotiate volume contracts with voice, telecom, and automation vendors.

### Stage 4: 50+ clients

- Create industry pods and formal SRE/on-call coverage.
- Add enterprise security, procurement, SLA, and compliance capability.
- Consider multi-tenant product components, but keep client data and secrets isolated.
- Expand geographically through partners and localized voice/telecom relationships.

## Key company metrics

- Monthly recurring revenue and annual recurring revenue.
- New setup revenue and setup gross margin.
- Direct recurring gross margin by client.
- Logo churn below 2% monthly.
- Net revenue retention above 110% as locations and modules expand.
- Customer acquisition payback below six months.
- Deployment time below 15 business days for standard clients.
- Core workflow success above 90% and improving.
- Support hours per client and incidents per 1,000 conversations.
- Platform cost per completed business outcome—not just per minute.

## Risks and controls

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| Vendor price changes | Quarterly cost review, 30-day repricing clause, multi-provider architecture where justified |
| Hallucinated menus/prices/policies | Source-controlled knowledge, retrieval tests, deterministic tools, escalation |
| Duplicate transactions | Idempotency key, confirmation state, server-side uniqueness and retry handling |
| Poor audio/noise | Denoising, microphone guidance, interruption tuning, PSTN-quality testing, human fallback |
| Client sees only a software bill | Monthly ROI report, business review, roadmap, completed-outcome metrics |
| Unlimited support destroys margin | Included hours, severity definitions, change packs, scoped projects |
| Shared infrastructure leaks data | Per-client secrets, row-level security, isolated production projects for premium clients |
| Pakistan telecom cost makes minutes unprofitable | Web voice first, local SIP/BYOC, itemized pass-through |

## Immediate 90-day execution plan

### Days 1–30

- Finalize the Launch and Growth packages.
- Build a restaurant ROI calculator and discovery form.
- Create 100 adversarial Urdu/Roman Urdu/English evaluation conversations.
- Produce a launch checklist, incident runbook, and monthly ROI report template.
- Select transactional email and monitoring standards.

### Days 31–60

- Recruit three paid design partners in one city/segment.
- Baseline missed calls, conversion, orders, reservations, and labor.
- Run weekly quality reviews and publish the first quantified case study.
- Establish one POS/restaurant consultant referral partnership.

### Days 61–90

- Convert successful pilots to six- or twelve-month contracts.
- Hire/assign a delivery QA owner.
- Launch partner commission: 10%–15% of first-year recurring revenue, paid only after client payment.
- Review real gross margin and update tier allowances.
- Begin outbound to 50 tightly qualified accounts per week.

## Final recommendation

Use the **Growth tier at $997/month globally or PKR 225,000/month locally** as the main offer, not the cheapest tier. It gives enough budget for reliable operations, real support, and measurable improvement while remaining far below custom AI project pricing and comparable managed reception services. Lead with a one-location paid pilot, prove completed orders/reservations and revenue, then expand by location and channel.
